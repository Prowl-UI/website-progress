Government of Kenya

Kenya is a democratic republic governed by the 2010 Constitution, which established a devolved system of government with both national and county levels of administration.

President of the Republic

His Excellency [President's Name]

President and Commander-in-Chief of the Kenya Defence Forces

The President is the Head of State and Government, elected directly by the people for a five-year term, with a maximum of two terms. The President exercises executive authority and is responsible for ensuring the protection of the sovereignty of Kenya and the rule of law.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Head of State and Government
  • Commander-in-Chief of Defence Forces
  • Chairperson of National Security Council
  • Symbol of national unity

Term Information:

  • Term: 5 years (max 2 terms)
  • Election: Direct popular vote
  • Threshold: 50%+1 of valid votes
  • Geographic spread requirement

System of Government

Presidential System

Kenya operates a presidential system where the President serves as both Head of State and Head of Government, with executive powers clearly separated from legislative and judicial branches.

Democratic Republic

A multi-party democracy where sovereignty belongs to the people, exercised through regular, free, and fair elections with universal adult suffrage.

Devolved Government

Power is shared between national and county governments to ensure services are delivered closer to the people and promote local development.

Levels of Government

National Government

The National Government handles matters of national importance and those specifically assigned by the Constitution, including foreign policy, national defense, currency, and immigration.

Key Functions:

  • Foreign affairs and international trade
  • National defense and security
  • Immigration and citizenship
  • Currency, banking, and monetary policy
  • National economic planning
  • Education standards and universities
  • Health policy and national referral facilities
  • National transport and communications
  • Natural resources and environmental conservation
  • Elections and electoral systems

Structure:

  • Executive: President, Deputy President, Cabinet
  • Legislature: National Assembly & Senate
  • Judiciary: Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, High Court

County Governments (47 Counties)

Kenya has 47 County Governments that provide services closer to the people and promote local development. Each county is led by an elected Governor and has its own assembly.

Key Functions:

  • Agriculture, livestock, and fisheries
  • County health services
  • Pre-primary education and village polytechnics
  • County roads and transport
  • Water and sanitation services
  • County planning and development
  • Trade development and regulation
  • County public works
  • Fire fighting and disaster management
  • Cultural activities and sports

Structure:

  • Executive: Governor, Deputy Governor, County Executive Committee
  • Legislature: County Assembly
  • Representation: 47 Counties across Kenya

The 2010 Constitution

Kenya's Supreme Law

Kenya is currently governed by the Constitution of Kenya 2010, which was promulgated on 27th August 2010 following a national referendum. This Constitution replaced the Independence Constitution of 1963 and established the current devolved system of government.

Key Features:
  • Bill of Rights with expanded fundamental freedoms
  • Devolved system with 47 county governments
  • Strengthened separation of powers
  • Independent judiciary and electoral commission
  • Enhanced public participation in governance
Core Principles:
  • Democracy and participation of the people
  • Human dignity, equity, and social justice
  • Inclusiveness and non-discrimination
  • Good governance, integrity, and accountability
  • Sustainable development
