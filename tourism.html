Tourism Overview

Tourism is one of the most important socio-economic sectors in Kenya, contributing Kshs.790 billion to the country's GDP and employing about 1.1 million people in the industry.

World Recognition

  • 37.33% growth in international arrivals (2018)

Economic Impact

  • 5.6% sector growth in 2019
  • 1.1 million people employed
  • 2,025,206 international arrivals (2018)

Tourism Opportunities & Products

Come Live the Magic

Wildlife & Safari Experiences

The Big Five

Home to the legendary Big 5 - Elephant, Rhino, Buffalo, Lion and Leopard. Kenya is the only country with a wildlife park within its capital city (Nairobi National Park).

The Special Five

Northern Kenya's unique wildlife - the Gerenuk, Somali Ostrich, Reticulated Giraffe, Grevy's Zebra and the fringe-eared Oryx.

Birding Paradise

Over 1,100 recorded species of birds. Prime locations include Lake Baringo, Lake Nakuru and Lake Elementaita.

Twin Migration

World-renowned for the twin migration of Humpback whales and Wildebeests. The Maasai Mara migration is highly sought after by international travelers.

Adventure Tourism

Trekking & Hiking

Chyulu Hills, Aberdares Ranges, Loroghi Hills, Matthews Ranges, and Ndoto Mountains offer spectacular trekking experiences.

Lake Turkana

World's largest permanent desert lake and alkaline lake. Experience cultural festivals and unique desert lake shores.

Health & Wellness

Natural hot springs and wellness tourism opportunities requiring development for international markets.

Medical Tourism

Nairobi has excellent medical facilities with potential to draw medical tourists from neighboring countries. Development opportunities exist for specialized medical tourism packages.

Year-Round Destination

Kenya offers unique experiences beyond migration season. Destination management companies provide custom-made itineraries for diverse traveler preferences.

Proudly Kenyan - Cultural Heritage

Kenya is a country of deeply rooted traditions and vibrant cultural cross-roads. Some of the oldest evidence of human culture has been discovered in Kenya, making it truly a cradle of humanity.

Ancient Heritage

Kenya is truly a cradle of humanity with some of the oldest evidence of human culture discovered here. The country links its prehistoric past to new cultural expressions in a land of breathtaking beauty.

Cultural Diversity

Kenya's diverse landscape - from snow-capped mountains to the Great Rift Valley, deserts to lakes, vast savannahs, lush forests, and sparkling coast - are reflected in the diversity of Kenyan people and their traditions.

Living Traditions

Many communities thrive in areas that have sustained their unique ways of life for centuries, while others are adapting their customs in growing urban areas.

Modern Expression

In rapidly growing urban centers, people of diverse cultures intersect, resulting in dynamic and creative new forms of expression that further enrich contemporary Kenyan culture.

Sports Excellence

Since the 1960s, Kenya's dominance in sports has been unrivaled. Currently ranked world's number two in IAAF World Championships and home to Eliud Kipchoge, the greatest marathoner of all time who ran under two hours in the INEOS challenge (1:59) in Vienna, Austria in 2019. Kenya is truly Africa's rising star!

Tourism Agencies & Support

Kenya's tourism sector is supported by various government and private agencies working together to promote sustainable tourism development and marketing.

Government Agencies

Ministry of Tourism and Wildlife, Kenya Tourism Board (KTB), and other regulatory bodies overseeing tourism development and promotion.

Industry Associations

Kenya Association of Tour Operators (KATO), Kenya Association of Hotel Keepers & Caterers (KAHC), and other industry bodies.

Marketing & Promotion

Destination marketing organizations and promotional agencies working to position Kenya as a premier tourism destination globally.

Investment Support

The government provides various incentives and support mechanisms for tourism investors, including streamlined licensing processes, tax incentives, and infrastructure development partnerships. Contact the embassy for detailed information on investment opportunities and support services.
