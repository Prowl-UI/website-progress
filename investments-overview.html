Investment Overview

Kenya is East and Central Africa's largest economy with strong growth prospects, offering investors strategic advantages and access to regional markets.

Why Invest in Kenya?

East & Central Africa's Largest Economy

Kenya is the largest and most advanced economy in East and Central Africa, with strong growth prospects supported by an emerging urban middle class and increasing appetite for high-value goods and services.

Low Risk Investment Environment

Kenya's investment climate is the strongest in the EAC, with FDI flowing from emerging and developed markets and a high volume of multinational companies with regional headquarters in the country.

Strategic Geographical Location

Kenya's geographical location makes it ideal for strategic partnerships aimed at improving regional and global market share. The country is the logistics, telecoms, financial and diplomatic hub for the region.

Skilled & Educated Talent Pool

Kenya prides itself on its large, highly educated and skilled workforce - with 55% of the population aged 15-64. The workforce is highly trainable and English-speaking.

Additional Investment Advantages

Political Stability

Empowered by a new constitution, national and county governments work with private sector as central development partners.

Improving Infrastructure

Over USD 20 billion committed towards infrastructure development through public private partnerships.

Green Energy Focus

Kenya is positioned to unleash Africa's power generation capacity through green energy, contributing 5000MW to the national grid.

Well Established Private Sector

Substantial private sector with foreign investors offering strong candidates for joint ventures and partnerships.

Good Liveability Factors

Nairobi ranked 6th in world's most dynamic cities with quality tourism, hospitality, and projected fastest-growing population.

Wide Market Access

East African Community (EAC)

  • Member Countries: 6
  • Population: 174 million
  • Total GDP: $1,172 billion

COMESA

  • Member Countries: 21
  • Population: 560 million
  • Total GDP: USD 769 billion

Tripartite (EAC-SADC-COMESA)

Potential market of over 600 million people through the Tripartite Free Trade Area cooperation.

Preferential Trade Treatment

  • Africa Growth and Opportunity Act (AGOA)
  • World Trade Organization
  • EAC-EU Trade Agreement
  • Africa Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA)

Kenya Vision 2030 & The Big 4 Agenda

Vision 2030

Kenya's Vision 2030 aims to transform the country into a newly industrializing, middle-income country providing a high quality of life to all its citizens by 2030 in a clean and secure environment. The Big 4 Agenda focuses on Manufacturing, Food Security, Universal Health Coverage, and Affordable Housing.
