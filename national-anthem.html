Kenya National Anthem - "Ee Mungu Nguvu Yetu"

The National Anthem of Kenya is "Ee Mungu Nguvu Yetu" (Oh God of All Creation). It was adopted in 1963 upon Kenya's independence and reflects the hopes and aspirations of the Kenyan people.

English Version

Verse 1:

Oh God of all creation
Bless this our land and nation
Justice be our shield and defender
May we dwell in unity
Peace and liberty
Plenty be found within our borders

Verse 2:

Let one and all arise
With hearts both strong and true
Service be our earnest endeavour
And our homeland of Kenya
Heritage of splendour
Firm may we stand to defend

Verse 3:

Let all with one accord
In common bond united
Build this our nation together
And the glory of Kenya
The fruit of our labour
Fill every heart with thanksgiving

About the Anthem

The music was composed by a joint commission with members from Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and Zambia. The lyrics reflect the hopes and aspirations of the Kenyan people for unity, peace, and prosperity.

Key Themes:

  • Unity and brotherhood among all Kenyans
  • Justice and peace
  • Service to the nation
  • Gratitude and thanksgiving
  • Pride in Kenya's heritage

East African Community (EAC) Anthem

As a member of the East African Community, Kenya also recognizes the EAC Anthem, which symbolizes the unity and cooperation among East African nations.

English Version

Verse 1:

Come one, come all
Arise and unite
East Africa is our land
East Africa is our home
From the source of the Nile
Down to the Indian Ocean
One people with one destiny

Chorus:

East Africa we want to unite
East Africa we want to unite
In work and in prosperity
We the people of East Africa

Verse 2:

Peacefully we want to develop
Our land and our nation
Let our hearts be united
Now and forever more
East Africa is our heritage
God bless our land
God bless East Africa

About the EAC Anthem

The East African Community Anthem represents the shared aspirations of the EAC Partner States: Burundi, Democratic Republic of Congo, Kenya, Rwanda, South Sudan, Tanzania, and Uganda.

EAC Vision:

  • Regional integration and cooperation
  • Peaceful development and prosperity
  • Unity among East African peoples
  • Shared heritage and destiny
  • Economic and social development

Musical Heritage

Both the Kenyan National Anthem and the EAC Anthem are important symbols of national and regional identity. They are performed at official ceremonies, sporting events, and diplomatic functions, reflecting Kenya's commitment to both national sovereignty and regional cooperation in East Africa.
