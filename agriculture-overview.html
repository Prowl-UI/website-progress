Agriculture in Kenya

Agriculture plays a pivotal role in Kenya's economic development as evidenced by its 26% direct contribution to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and 27% indirect contribution to the GDP through linkages with other sectors.

The sector is also a major source of livelihood through its employment of more than 40% of the total population and more than 70% of Kenya's rural people. The sector has continued to grow at almost 5% per annum and has a huge potential for further growth.

Key Exports

Horticulture, Coffee, Tea, Pulses, Pyrethrum Extract, and Sisal Products account for over 90% of agricultural exports

Major Agricultural Sectors

Tea & Coffee

Kenya is the world's largest tea exporter and produces premium Arabica coffee renowned globally for its quality and unique flavor profile.

  • Third largest tea producer globally
  • 100% Mild Arabica coffee
  • Premium specialty markets

Horticulture

Leading exporter of cut flowers, fruits, and vegetables to Europe and other international markets with year-round production capability.

  • Third largest flower exporter
  • Premium vegetables & herbs
  • Year-round production

Livestock

One of the largest dairy producers in Sub-Saharan Africa with diverse livestock including cattle, goats, and camels providing milk and meat.

  • 5.7 billion litres milk annually
  • 3.5M improved dairy cattle
  • Growing export market

Fruits & Nuts

Growing sector with macadamia nuts, avocados, mangoes, and passion fruits. Kenya is the third largest macadamia exporter globally.

  • 12% annual growth (2005-2015)
  • Premium macadamia quality
  • Processing opportunities

Pyrethrum

Natural insecticide from chrysanthemum flowers. Kenyan pyrethrin is considered the best in the world with ideal growing conditions.

  • Best quality globally
  • 20,000 MT potential annually
  • 19 producing counties

Agribusiness

Supporting infrastructure and services for agricultural development including processing, storage, and value addition opportunities.

  • Value addition focus
  • Modern processing facilities
  • Export market access

Investment Opportunities

Kenya's agriculture sector offers numerous investment opportunities across the value chain from production to processing and export. The government provides various incentives and support for agricultural investments.

Key Opportunities

  • Cold chain and storage facilities
  • Irrigation and modern farming
  • Organic and specialty products
  • Export market development

Government Support

  • Market access facilitation
