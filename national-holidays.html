National Holidays

Kenya observes several national holidays throughout the year that commemorate important historical events, cultural traditions, and religious observances.

Public Holidays

Date Holiday Description
January 1 New Year's Day Celebration of the beginning of the new year
June 1 Madaraka Day Commemorates Kenya's attainment of self-rule in 1963
October 10 Huduma Day Day dedicated to public service and community service
October 20 Mashujaa Day Heroes' Day honoring those who fought for Kenya's independence
December 12 Jamhuri Day Republic Day celebrating Kenya's independence in 1963
December 25 Christmas Day Christian celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ
December 26 Boxing Day Day of giving and charity following Christmas

Variable Religious Holidays

  • Good Friday - Friday before Easter Sunday
  • Easter Monday - Monday after Easter Sunday
  • Eid al-Fitr - End of Ramadan (Islamic calendar)
  • Eid al-Adha - Festival of Sacrifice (Islamic calendar)
  • Diwali - Hindu festival of lights
Note: When a public holiday falls on a Sunday, the following Monday is observed as a holiday. Embassy services are closed on all public holidays.
