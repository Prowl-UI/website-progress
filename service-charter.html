Citizen's Service Delivery Charter

Foreword

Service charters have proved to be very important tools in any organization in instilling confidence in customers and demonstrate a competitive edge and promote a commitment to cordial relations. However, a service charter can become a burden if the organization fails to deliver on customer expectations.

It is therefore the desire of the Kenya Embassy in Bangkok to use this 'social contract' to guide the staff in ensuring effective service delivery to the satisfaction of our esteemed customers in line with the 'mother' charter of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Kenya.

The aim is to ensure that the Bangkok Mission contributes effectively to the achievement of the national development agenda of a peaceful, competitive and prosperous Kenya as espoused in the Kenya 2010, Kenya Vision 2030, MTP and the Bottom up Transformation Agenda (BETA).

Purpose of the Charter

The purpose of the Charter is to inform our customers and stakeholders of our commitment to executing our duties promptly, efficiently and professionally to meet the expectations of both Kenyan citizens and other clients.

The charter defines who we are, what we stand for, the services and the standards of services provided.

Introduction

The Embassy of the Republic of Kenya in Bangkok, Thailand, was established in 2006 as the focal point of Kenya's bilateral engagements with Thailand, Viet Nam, Cambodia, Lao PDR and Myanmar.

The Mission, guided by various institutional frameworks including the foreign policy document and the Ministry's Strategic plan 2018/2019-2022/2023, continues to engage the five ASEAN countries to promote, protect and project Kenya's national interests and image.

Vision

A model Diplomatic Mission contributing to a peaceful, prosperous and globally competitive Kenya

Mission

To articulate and implement Kenya's foreign policy in Thailand, Myanmar, Cambodia, Lao PDR and Viet Nam through innovative and effective diplomatic engagements.

Core Values

  • Patriotism, national unity, rule of law, and customer focus
  • Human rights, equality, and equity
  • Integrity, transparency and accountability
  • Professionalism
  • Sustainable Development
